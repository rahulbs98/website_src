About Me

I am a cryptography researcher. I am currently interning at NTT Research, Tokyo with Prof. Masayuki Abe. I am also pursuing my PhD at IT University of Copenhagen, Denmark with Prof. Bernardo David. I work in the field of Applied Cryptography, focusing on Secure Multiparty Computation, Zero Knowledge Proofs, and Privacy Enhancing Technologies. I aim to develop concretely efficient techniques and bridge the gap between theory and practice.

I have a Master's in Computer Engineering from Bar-Ilan University, Israel and completed my undergraduate studies at BITS Pilani, Goa with a double major in Math and Electronics.

My Master's thesis at The Center for Research in Applied Cryptography and Cyber Security, Bar Ilan University was supervised by Prof. Carmit Hazay, focusing on circumventing lower bounds in garbled circuits.

I have previously worked as a consultant researcher in the Privacy And Scaling Explorations Group at Ethereum Foundation and at Chain Reaction, Israel. Additionally, I have been a cryptography research intern at QED-it and the Blockchain and Smart Contracts Group at IBM Research India Labs.
